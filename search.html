Initial Markup (IMU%) is essentially the amount you add on top of the cost.


Gross Margin Rate (GM%) is essentially the IMU% less markdowns. This is often a target merchandise planners and finance teams align on to ensure the company will essentially be able to survive. Planners often stop here with the exception of inventory managment. Finance will focus more on operational numbers without necessarily being involved with the day to day of the product. The partnership is quite heavy between finance and planning. Planners have to be strategic, financially savvy, and concious of the product, marketing, competetitors, and so much more all at the same time. Time periods reviewed are past, present, future and often at the same time. Buyers and Planners go hand and hand and can sometimes switch roles. A skilled Buyer or Planner can often step into either role.


Net Profit is more of a financial term which essentially is money left over after everything is complete. Understanding this number will help you understand the importance of IMU.
